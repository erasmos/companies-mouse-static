When a company goes into a receivership, a receiver is appointed to sell one or more secured assets, in order to maximum the debt repaid to a particular secured creditor (usually a bank); it is usually this secured creditor who initiates the process. Note that the company can continue to trade if the receiver thinks it will benefit the secured creditor. Receivership has become quite rare these days, as going into administration has become favoured. In this case, the company is still allowed to trade, although limited by the inaccessibility of the assets under the receiver's control.
