When a company goes into administration - whether voluntarily or as ordered by the court - the directors give full control of their company to a number of assigned insolvency practitioners, with the goals of preventing it from going into liquidation, or at least getting a better result for all creditors than if it went into immediate liquidation. During a period (usually eight weeks) the company is protected from legal action. Note that the company can only continue trading if the insolvency practitioners think it will support these goals.
Note that this is just a random sampling.
