When a company goes into a receivership, a receiver is appointed to sell one or more secured assets, in order to maximum the debt repaid to a particular secured creditor (usually a bank); it is usually this secured creditor who initiates the process. Note that the company can continue to trade if the receiver thinks it will benefit the secured creditor; but note that the trading must proceed without the benefit of the asset(s) under the receiver's control. Receivership has become quite rare these days, as going into administration has become favoured. In this case, the company is not allowed to trade.
